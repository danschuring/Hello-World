Introduction

Most of us may want to do whatâ€™s best for the environment, but the perceived hurdles to making environmentally friendly changes can make it difficult. New technology may cost more upfront, may have certain environmental downsides (fluorescent lights contain mercury for example), or may not provide the same quality (fluorescent lighting can be harsh). But, lighting turned a corner with LEDs (light-emitting diode bulbs). They are more efficient than even fluorescent bulbs, and the quality of light is about the same as incandescent bulbs. The only thing standing in your way may be the costs of the LED bulbs which can be three to five or more times the expense of incandescent bulbs. If you do the math though, it becomes a no-brainer to make the switch in most cases, even from fluorescents. If youâ€™re on a tight budget, replace a few bulbs a month with the efficient LEDs, starting with the lights you use most often.
